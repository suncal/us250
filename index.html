250 Years in the Making

A once-in-a-lifetime celebration of the American story.

The U.S. 250 Tour is a monumental, coast-to-coast journey celebrating the 250th anniversary of the United States of America. As we reflect on two and a half centuries of American ingenuity, faith, and freedom, this tour brings history to life through music, storytelling, education, and family-centered activities.

Kicking off in California and traveling to all 50 states — with 26 flagship locations of historical significance — every event is a full day of patriotic experiences: local historians, high-profile speakers, patriotic concerts, and the immersive Freedom Village. Entirely free to the public.

"An opportunity to celebrate the past, honor the present, and inspire a shared vision for the future."
— The U.S. 250 Tour Mission

The Inspiration Behind the Tour

The Friendship Train — a bright light in America's history.

The year was 1947. The world was still recovering from the ravages of World War II, and Europe was hit especially hard. American journalist Drew Pearson, touring post-war Europe, watched a few carloads of grain win hearts — and knew America could do better.

What followed became one of the great untold stories of American generosity — a train that grew as it crossed the nation, carrying the goodwill of ordinary people. Its message was laid in granite on a little-known monument in Kailua, Hawai'i, so we would remember the miracle of America.
